# Evaluation Instructions: ReadingMachine Open Review

## Purpose of This Evaluation

This evaluation assesses the epistemic fidelity of ReadingMachine’s structured corpus reading. 

**The paper for evaluation can be found [here](https://github.com/morrisseyj/ReadingMachine/blob/main/evaluation/industrial_policy_main_run/industrial_policy_current.md).**

**The form for completing the evaluation can be found [here](https://forms.gle/z3Mxpu8mFnj6xoRp8)**

Specifically, reviewers are asked to evaluate whether the ReadingMachine output provides a faithful, coherent, and usable mapping of the arguments, themes, and disagreements present in the corpus it was given.

The evaluation is not intended to judge the output as:

- an argument
- a position paper
- a narrative review
- or a piece of prose writing

Instead, it evaluates the output as an intermediate analytical representation: a structured reading of a large body of text, produced prior to any downstream reasoning, interpretation, or advocacy. For more details on the tool's methodology see the [whitepaper](https://github.com/morrisseyj/ReadingMachine/blob/main/documentation/white_paper.md).

---

## What You Are Being Asked to Evaluate

When completing the evaluation form, please focus on the following questions:

- Does the output cover the major thematic strands of the literature, to the extent that you would expect given your familiarity with the field?
- Are arguments, debates, and conflicts represented in ways that are recognizable and not distorted?
- Are themes conceptually coherent, or do distinct debates appear incorrectly merged?
- Are there any obvious hallucinations or misattributed references?
- Do the themes meaningfully address the stated research questions?
- Is the output usable as a basis for further analysis, synthesis, or decision‑making?

You are not expected to know the entire corpus exhaustively, and you are not expected to verify every claim or citation.

---

## What Is Explicitly Out of Scope

Please do not evaluate:

- prose quality, style, or tone
- whether you agree with the positions reflected in the output
- whether conclusions are politically, morally, or normatively correct
- whether you would frame the literature differently yourself

This evaluation is about fidelity and structure, not persuasion.

---

## Open, Attributed Review

This is an open, non‑anonymous review process.

When submitting a review, you will be asked to provide:

- your name
- your email address - This will not be public but may be used to verify your review, prevent duplicate reviews and allow clarrification if needed.-
- your affiliation or role
- your primary domain(s) of expertise
- your self‑assessed familiarity with the relevant literature

This information is used to contextualize evaluation results, not to weight, rank, or exclude reviews.

Disagreement among reviewers is expected and informative.

---

## Partial Completion Is Expected and Valid

You are not required to complete all sections of the evaluation form.

Each evaluation dimension is independent. You are encouraged to:

- answer only those questions you feel qualified to assess
- select “Not assessed” where appropriate

Because no reviewer can be expected to know the full corpus, partial assessments are treated as meaningful contributions rather than incomplete responses.

## How the Evaluation Form Is Structured

The evaluation form is organized into independent sections.

---

### Section 0: Reviewer Context

You will be asked to identify yourself and briefly describe your domain expertise and familiarity with the relevant literature.

This provides context for interpreting aggregated results.

---

### Section 1: Thematic Coverage & Alignment

These questions ask whether, within your knowledge of the field:

- major thematic strands are present
- themes meaningfully address the stated research questions

You are not being asked to judge exhaustive completeness.

### Section 2: Conceptual Integrity

These questions ask whether:

- themes are internally coherent
- distinct debates appear improperly merged
- arguments are flattened or distorted during synthesis

This is about conceptual structure, not wording.

---

### Section 3: Factual & Referential Integrity

These questions focus narrowly on:

- obvious hallucinations (claims that clearly do not exist in the literature)
- implausible or clearly incorrect attribution of ideas to sources

You are not expected to fact‑check every citation.

### Section 4: Conflict Representation

These questions assess whether:

- real disagreements in the literature are surfaced where expected
- conflict is preserved rather than collapsed into false consensus

This dimension is especially important given ReadingMachine’s design goals.

---

### Section 5: Analytical Usefulness (Optional)

This section asks whether, taken as a structured representation of the literature, the output is usable as a basis for:

- further research
- policy analysis
- downstream interpretation or reasoning

This is a pragmatic assessment, not a normative one.

### Section 6: Overall Assessment

You may provide an overall judgment of epistemic fidelity as a corpus reading, as well as optional additional comments.

---

## How Evaluation Results Are Used

Evaluation responses are aggregated by dimension, not collapsed into a single score.

For each evaluation question, we report:

- how many reviewers assessed that dimension
- the distribution of responses
- the number of reviewers who marked “Not assessed”

Selected attributed comments may be quoted to illustrate recurring strengths or concerns.

Evaluation results are used to:

- identify failure modes
- assess robustness across corpora
- guide further methodological development

They are not used to claim definitive correctness or consensus.

## Thank You

By participating in this evaluation, you are contributing to an experiment in transparent, inspectable, large‑scale reading.

Thoughtful partial feedback is far more valuable than forced completeness.
